# junyanxiong
## Problem Statement
1.  What are the key differences in the historical stock price growth trends of Nike and Under Armour, and what factors drive these divergent patterns.
2.  How have partnerships with high-profile athletes and industry figures influenced the brand image, market performance, and ultimately the stock returns of both companies.

## Data & Methodology
- Data Source: WRDS (Wharton Research Data Services)
- Workflow:
  1. Extract and clean stock price/return data for NKE and UAA (2015–2018)
  2. Visualize long-term price and return trends
  3. Calculate key metrics: annualized volatility, Sharpe ratio, and maximum drawdown
  4. Analyze short-term stock returns during NBA Finals periods

  ## Key Findings
1.  **Long-Term Performance**: Nike consistently outperformed Under Armour across all key financial metrics (returns, volatility-adjusted performance, and downside risk) over the 2015–2018 period. This gap is largely attributable to Nike’s larger scale, diversified revenue streams, and stronger market position.
2.  **Celebrity Endorsement Impact**: Despite the difference in firm size, high-profile endorsements by LeBron James (Nike) and Stephen Curry (Under Armour) delivered measurable, short-term positive impacts on both companies’ stock prices during the NBA Finals. The analysis confirms that star endorsements can drive significant investor attention and price movements, regardless of the company’s overall market capitalization.
3.  **Broader Implication**: While scale provides inherent advantages, strategic celebrity partnerships remain a powerful tool for enhancing brand visibility and creating tangible short-term value, offering a clear cost-benefit consideration for firms evaluating endorsement investments.
